Youtube Channels

Here is a list of math and science Youtube channels that I like to watch. I put them here in case they might be interesting to you also! Youtube has been a great resource for me to not only get entertainment from, but to learn from.

Numberphile

Of course, Numberphile has to be the first one on the list. According to their about page on Youtube, they make "Videos about numbers - it's that simple." Brady Haran makes a ton of interesting math videos with a ton of interesting math people. I will forever love Numberphile, as they are one of the reasons I first started to love math.

Coding Train

The Coding Train makes "creative coding video tutorials on subjects ranging from the basics of programming languages like JavaScript to algorithmic art, machine learning, simulation, generative poetry, and more." Daniel Shiffman helped me get into programming, and he makes some really great and fun videos.

3Blue1Brown

3Blue1Brown is another great math channel ran by Grant Sanderson. According to its about page, "the goal is for explanations to be driven by animations and for difficult problems to be made simple with changes in perspective." The channel has a ton of interesting videos that look great, and it is one of my favorite math channels.

Sebastian Lague

Sebastian Lague makes videos where he "explores how to create stuff out of code." He makes some incredible simulations in Unity and explores some really cool concepts. While the videos might not be as easy to follow as the Coding Train, they are still very fun to watch.
